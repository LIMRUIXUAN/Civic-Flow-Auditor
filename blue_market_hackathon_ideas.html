Kaggle AI Agents Capstone - Blue-Market Stress Test

Seven underserved AI-agent project ideas worth testing now

Verdict: shortlist the top 3, build 1. Confidence: 72%.

The strongest hackathon bets are civic accessibility auditing, benefits recertification help, and job scam defense. They have visible pain, public data, agent-native workflows, and a credible demo path inside a four-day window.

Score Meters

Problem Evidence

Strong. Sources include FTC, ADA.gov, AHRQ, AMA, Michigan 211, Urban Institute, and nonprofit-sector reports.

Hackathon Fit

Good. Most can be built with document intake, retrieval, tool calls, scoring, and a human-readable action plan.

Blue-Market Gap

Mixed-to-strong. Some categories have incumbents, but the underserved wedge is narrower, local, and workflow-heavy.

TL;DR

  • Build #1 if you want the cleanest Kaggle story: Civic Access Auditor. It uses agents, public web data, compliance rules, and practical output.
  • Build #2 if you want social-impact weight: Benefits Churn Navigator. It targets people losing help because paperwork and notices are hard.
  • Build #3 if you want a sharp demo: Job Scam Shield. The before/after value is instantly understandable in a five-minute video.
  • Avoid overbuilding. The winning submission needs a working agentic workflow, not a full company.
  • Hard warning: healthcare, education, benefits, and legal-adjacent projects must be framed as decision support, not professional advice.

The Seven Ideas

01 Civic Access Auditor Best bet

Who hurts
Small city clerks, school districts, special districts, and residents with disabilities.
Unsolved problem
State and local government web content, mobile apps, and documents must meet WCAG 2.1 AA, but small entities lack audit capacity.
Agent build
Crawl a public site, inspect pages and PDFs, classify ADA risk, draft remediation tickets, suggest alt text, and produce a compliance roadmap.
Hackathon track
Agents for Good or Agents for Business.
48-hour test
Run on two small public sites and compare top issues against a manual checker. Success: 80% of top-10 issues are valid and actionable.

02 Benefits Churn Navigator High impact

Who hurts
SNAP, Medicaid, utility-assistance, and housing-aid applicants who miss renewal steps or cannot match documents to requirements.
Unsolved problem
Eligible people lose coverage or benefits because notices, interviews, work requirements, and recertification packets are confusing.
Agent build
Parse a notice, extract deadlines, ask for missing docs, create a checklist, connect to 211-style resources, and prepare a call script.
Hackathon track
Agents for Good or Concierge Agents.
48-hour test
Use five public/sample notices. Success: the agent finds all deadlines, required documents, and next actions without hallucinated eligibility claims.

03 Job Scam Shield Demo-friendly

Who hurts
Job seekers, new graduates, laid-off workers, international students, and remote-work applicants.
Unsolved problem
AI-made fake recruiters and polished job offers are harder to spot than old scam emails.
Agent build
Analyze job posts, recruiter messages, domains, payment requests, interview flow, and public company signals. Produce a risk score and verification script.
Hackathon track
Concierge Agents or Agents for Good.
48-hour test
Classify 20 known real/fake job examples. Success: 85%+ accuracy with explanation grounded in visible evidence.

04 Rental Proof Agent Clear pain

Who hurts
Renters searching on Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, and informal housing groups.
Unsolved problem
Fake listings copy real photos, change contact information, and pressure renters to pay before verification.
Agent build
Review listing text, address clues, duplicate photos, payment asks, ownership hints, and safe-tour requirements. Output a verification report.
Hackathon track
Concierge Agents.
48-hour test
Test 10 sample listings. Success: catches duplicated-photo and upfront-payment patterns while giving non-alarmist next steps.

05 Care Transition Copilot Scope carefully

Who hurts
Family caregivers taking someone home after a hospital stay.
Unsolved problem
Discharge paperwork is dense; people miss medication changes, follow-ups, warning signs, and who to call.
Agent build
Turn discharge instructions into a calendar, medication schedule, red-flag watchlist, and "questions for the nurse" checklist.
Hackathon track
Concierge Agents or Agents for Good.
48-hour test
Use synthetic discharge packets. Success: no medical advice, all tasks trace back to source text, and every risky item says "call clinician."

06 Grant Burden Reducer Business angle

Who hurts
Small nonprofits without dedicated grants staff.
Unsolved problem
Grant reports, budgets, attachments, metrics, and funder-specific formats consume hours that should go to programs.
Agent build
Ingest a funder report template plus program notes, receipts, and outcomes; draft the report, flag missing evidence, and maintain a deadline ledger.
Hackathon track
Agents for Business or Agents for Good.
48-hour test
Use three funder templates and one mock nonprofit dataset. Success: reduces report assembly time by 50% in a timed demo.

07 IEP Meeting Prep Agent Legal guardrails

Who hurts
Parents of students with disabilities, especially first-time IEP families and non-English-speaking households.
Unsolved problem
IEP meetings move quickly, documents are acronym-heavy, and parents often do not know what to ask or track.
Agent build
Summarize the IEP, compare goals year over year, translate jargon, generate questions, and create an action log after the meeting.
Hackathon track
Agents for Good or Concierge Agents.
48-hour test
Use two public/sample IEP-like documents. Success: finds goals, services, accommodations, missing dates, and parent questions without legal advice.

I scored instead for hackathon fit: a solo or small team, four days left, public/synthetic data, and a need to show at least three agent concepts such as ADK, MCP, security guardrails, deployment, and agent skills.

Capital And Resource Reality

Risk Reality check Scope control
Data privacy Benefits, health, education, and grant documents can contain sensitive information. Use synthetic/sample docs, local redaction, and a "do not upload secrets" warning.
Professional advice Some ideas border on legal, medical, benefits, or special education advice. Position as document organization and question generation; always recommend qualified human review.
Tool reliability Web search, OCR, and public data can be incomplete or stale. Show citations, confidence labels, and source snippets for every output.
Build time Full apps are too large for the remaining window. Ship one narrow workflow: intake, retrieval, agent reasoning, action plan, export.

Macro Risk Audit

Platform risk: Maybe

Search, LLM, browser, OCR, and platform APIs may change. Keep the demo portable and cite sources.

Regulatory wall: Yes

Healthcare, benefits, education, and accessibility all have compliance surfaces. Use synthetic data and strict disclaimers.

Commoditization: Maybe

Generic LLMs can summarize documents; the moat is workflow, grounding, checks, and domain-specific output.

Substitution: Maybe

Broad AI assistants and security tools will absorb simple scam checks. Narrow, auditable workflows still matter.

Cycle reversal: No

These are not ZIRP-only pains. Paperwork, scams, accessibility, and care coordination persist across cycles.

Devil's Advocate Findings

  • Prior authorization is painful but crowded. AMA data is brutal, but free tools like Fight Health Insurance and Counterforce already exist. Build only if you have a narrower clinic workflow.
  • Benefits navigation can accidentally overpromise. The agent must never say someone is eligible with certainty. It should say what documents and next steps are visible.
  • Healthcare and IEP ideas are trust-sensitive. Hallucinated advice can harm people. Use citations, source-grounding, and "ask a professional" handoff points.
  • Scam tools face a substitution clock. Norton-style scam detection is moving inside general AI assistants, so the defensible wedge is job/rental-specific verification workflow.
  • Accessibility auditing can become shallow. Automated checkers miss many human accessibility issues. Your demo should call this out and focus on triage, not full certification.
  • Grant tools are crowded at the top of funnel. Avoid "AI grant writer." Focus on post-award reporting and evidence tracking, where small nonprofits still drown.

Recommendation

Pick Civic Access Auditor unless you have a strong personal connection to another domain. It has a deadline-driven market, public inputs, visible outputs, lower personal-data risk, and a strong architecture story: crawler agent, accessibility-rule retrieval, PDF/OCR worker, remediation planner, and report generator.

Named experiment: the Two-Site Triage Test. Scan two small public agency sites, generate the top 10 issues per site, then manually verify with a browser accessibility checker and document review. Build only if at least 16 of 20 issues are real, source-linked, and phrased as clear tickets a clerk or developer could act on.
